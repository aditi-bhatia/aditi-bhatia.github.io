Lullabyte: An IoT Android Application

California aims to reduce the consumption of non-renewable energy sources and increase renewable energy sources with a target of 50% renewables by 2030. Simultaneously, Internet of Things (IoT) technology is growing in capability and popularity enabled by low cost, low power, microcontroller and wireless components. Can IoT technology be a major key to both energy savings and demand response? By 2020, IoT will be a trillion-dollar industry worldwide with a subnet of multi-billion-dollar energy management industry. What types of devices and which implementation strategies can yield the largest impact on energy management? We seek to deliver a solution to bring control to a class of overlooked large residential loads.

Our project aims to reduce and manage energy consumption within high load devices by monitoring energy usage and providing control of these devices in residences. These loads include pool pumps, spas, HVAC compressors, and Electric Vehicle (EV) chargers. Pool pumps commonly have two speed modes that can be controlled. In collaboration with Smartenit, LLC, we developed a triple relay load controller that interfaces through WiFi. The device is designed to measure and control 3 loads up to 240VAC and 30 AMPs, allowing consumers to manage large loads in an intelligent and efficient manner in order to maximize energy management.


Components of this project:
IoT-MLC, hardware triple relay load controller, includes a WiFi module, current/voltage (power) metering chip, efficient power supply, and relay drivers.

MQTT, lightweight push/subscribe messaging protocol, connects the IoT-MLC and controller devices thru a broker-based message transfer system.

Application, user interface implemented in another hardware device, enables the home user to have the ability to remotely control the IoT-MLC as well as display pertinent data obtained from the load controller. This system is currently implemented on an Android tablet.
